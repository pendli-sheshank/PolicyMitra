## Section 2: Waiting Periods

<!-- clause: CL-WAIT-INITIAL-30D -->
There is an initial waiting period of 30 days from the date of policy commencement for all illnesses and their complications, except for hospitalization arising from an accident, which is covered from day one of the policy.

## Section 3: Room Rent & Accommodation

<!-- clause: CL-ROOM-RENT -->
The Accommodation Charges Limit under this Policy permits a Single Private Room with no percentage-of-Sum-Insured capping on room rent, provided the room tariff does not exceed the hospital's standard Single Private Room category. There is no proportionate deduction applied to associated hospitalization expenses when the Insured Person stays within this category.

## Section 4: Co-payment

<!-- clause: CL-COPAY -->
No co-payment applies for treatment availed at a network hospital, regardless of the Insured Person's age. A co-payment of 20% of the admissible claim amount applies to treatment availed at a non-network hospital.

## Section 5: Exclusions

<!-- clause: CL-EXCL-COSMETIC -->
Cosmetic or plastic surgery, including any complications arising from such surgery, is excluded unless required to treat a covered accidental injury or as part of reconstructive surgery following a covered mastectomy or burn.

<!-- clause: CL-EXCL-OBESITY -->
Treatment for obesity or weight-loss (bariatric) surgery is excluded unless the treating doctor certifies the surgery is medically necessary due to a Body Mass Index (BMI) of 40 or above, or a BMI of 35 or above with a related co-morbid condition.

<!-- clause: CL-EXCL-WAR -->
Any injury, illness, or death directly or indirectly caused by war, invasion, act of a foreign enemy, or nuclear, chemical, or biological attack is excluded.

## Section 6: Network Hospitals

<!-- clause: CL-NETWORK -->
The Policy provides cashless treatment access at 6,100 network hospitals across India. Treatment at a non-network hospital is reimbursed at the applicable rates, subject to the 20% co-payment described in Section 4.

## Section 7: Portability

<!-- clause: CL-PORTABILITY -->
In accordance with IRDAI portability regulations, an Insured Person porting into this Policy from another health insurance policy shall be given credit for the time already served against the waiting periods for pre-existing diseases and specific illnesses, up to the waiting periods specified in Section 2 of this Policy, provided the porting application is made within the prescribed window before the expiry of the existing policy and there has been no break in coverage.
